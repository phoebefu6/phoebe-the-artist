# The average customer is a myth

## Key Concept

Averages can turn many incompatible customer realities into one fake persona. The mean may look calm in an executive summary, while the distribution hides margin leakage, support burden, churn risk, discounting, and outlier accounts.

## Explanation

The marble statue represents the tidy fiction of the "average customer." It looks stable, classical, and decision-ready. But when it cracks open, the inside is not a single customer at all - it is a messy pile of receipts, invoices, tickets, discounts, churn notes, premium signals, and loss markers.

The visual argues that executives should not decide from one comfortable number. They should inspect the spread, the segments, and the tail where the real business consequence lives.
